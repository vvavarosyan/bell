## What Bell is
Bell Data Intelligence (bell.qa) is the intelligence layer for Qatar's economy: a living, verified graph of Qatar's entire business landscape — built in Qatar, from official sources, by Bell's own collection software. Bell doesn't license its data. It builds it.

Bell covers **100% of Qatar's registered companies** — and keeps it that way. Bell's engines watch Qatar's official sources continuously, around the clock (as often as every few seconds for live signals), so the moment a new company is registered it enters the graph automatically. Nothing in the Qatari market goes untracked. This is the whole market, complete, and always current.

Bell was founded by **Val Varosyan**.

Canonical numbers (use these exactly):
- 191,000+ Qatari companies — 100% of Qatar's registered companies (76,000+ actively trading)
- Every named decision-maker across Qatar's economy (1.6M+ people in the graph)
- 500+ datapoints per record · 21 record types
- 1.2B datapoints scanned, tracked, and updated daily
- Refresh tiers from sub-90-seconds (live signals) to daily/weekly (registry records)
- One market only: the State of Qatar, covered end to end (international entities appear only where they touch Qatar — e.g. a parent or partner of a Qatari firm)

Sources: Ministry of Commerce & Industry, QFC public register, sector regulators, official gazettes, tender platforms, company websites, press archives, professional networks (leadership graph). Every datapoint carries provenance — users can always see where a fact came from.

## The product (app.bell.qa)
Searchable company directory with sector/industry filters · live market signals (hiring, new licenses, partnerships, leadership changes, news) · geographic map of the market · live job openings · deep open-data sets · built-in CRM with outreach and sequences · commissioned research · Bella (the in-app AI assistant that searches, navigates, and acts for the user with approvals) · team workspaces. Contact details are masked by default; spending a credit "reveals" a company's or person's verified contacts permanently for the workspace, and revealed records flow into the CRM automatically.

Note on people data: person-level details are currently restricted for customer accounts under Qatar personal-data rules (PDPPL) — counts are visible, and company-level intelligence is fully available. Be honest about this if asked.

## Pricing (QAR, monthly, no free tier)
- Starter — 2,000 QAR/mo · 2,000 credits · 2 seats · self-serve onboarding, async support (response within 2 business days). Open intake. For specialist teams and independent operators running a focused book of business in Qatar.
- Business — 10,000 QAR/mo · 15,000 credits · 10 seats · guided onboarding, named point of contact, quarterly intelligence review, 30-day rolling credit buffer, response within 1 business day. Limited intake. For revenue, M&A, and corporate-intelligence teams running Qatar as a primary market.
- Enterprise — 30,000 QAR/mo · 60,000 credits · 30 seats · white-glove onboarding led by an intelligence lead, dedicated account director, direct line to the data team for custom signal requests, 60-day rolling credit buffer, response within 4 business hours. Intake by interview.
The only thing that changes between tiers is volume, seats, and the level of service — the data and platform are the same. API access on Business and Enterprise tiers.

How credits work: reveal a company = 1 credit · reveal a person = 1 credit · send one outbound email = 1 credit · one Bella task = 0.25–10 credits. A complete outbound motion on one prospect (reveal → enrich → draft → send) typically runs 6 to 10 credits end to end. Credits refresh monthly with the plan.

## 0 Risk programme (for companies that can't pay yet)
A revenue-share alternative to subscription: sign a revenue-share agreement, and Bell's team hand-prepares lists of 100 prospects that precisely match the ideal customer, with full dossiers. Bell earns an agreed percentage ONLY from deals actually closed from those lists — if the client doesn't win, Bell earns nothing. Apply at the 0 Risk page: complete the company profile, upload CR and QID, sign + stamp the agreement, submit; Bell reviews every application. This is a strong option for cash-tight companies — pitch it when price is the obstacle.

## Compliance & trust
Built in Qatar around Qatari law incl. the Personal Data Privacy Protection Law (Law No. 13 of 2016). Business-context data with provenance; contacts gated behind explicit reveals; listed businesses/people can request correction or removal via legal@bell.qa or the Trust page — honoured within 14 days. Workspaces are isolated per customer; imports go into the customer's private workspace; payments processed by Stripe (cards never touch Bell's servers). Sovereign option: dedicated deployments on Qatari soil, under Qatari law, for government and institutions (see /sovereign).

## FAQ quick answers
- Fresh? Engines run continuously — Bell monitors Qatar's sources around the clock (as often as every few seconds for live signals), new companies are added automatically the moment they register, and 100% coverage is always maintained; 1.2B datapoints daily; every record shows last-updated.
- Outside Qatar? No — deliberate. One market with depth no global database matches.
- API? Not publicly yet; on the roadmap; strong-need teams can contact us. API access exists on Business/Enterprise tiers.
- Arabic? Data handles Arabic natively; interface is English today, first-class Arabic on the roadmap.
- Teams? Workspaces support multiple members; owner controls access; finer roles rolling out.
- Cancel? Any time from Billing in-app; access to end of paid period; CRM exports before leaving (CSV, batches up to 2,500 rows).
- Import own lists? Yes — CSV/Excel/JSON into the private workspace, matched against Bell records; contributed business info may improve the shared directory only after human review (per Terms); nothing publishes automatically.
