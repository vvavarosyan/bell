# Bell Data Intelligence — Brief for Legal Counsel

**Subject:** Lawfulness of (a) automated B2B marketing email and (b) a "gift-a-lead" mechanic, under Qatar's Personal Data Privacy Protection Law (Law No. 13 of 2016 / PDPPL) and the NCSA Direct-Marketing guidelines.

*Prepared by Bell Data Intelligence for review by Qatar-licensed counsel. This document is our understanding; we are seeking your legal opinion and required changes before launch.*

---

## 1. About Bell

Bell Data Intelligence (bell.qa) is a Qatar B2B data-intelligence SaaS. We compile a database of Qatar companies and their decision-makers from **public sources** (official business registers: QFZ, QFC/QFCRA, MOCI, QSTP, QSE, Qatar Chamber, CRA, Made in Qatar, MoPH, etc.) and enrich it (company websites, public profiles). Paid customers use Bell to find target companies, "reveal" contact details (charged per reveal), manage them in a CRM, and run outreach from **their own** verified email domain.

## 2. What we want to do (and need cleared)

Bell wants to market **itself** using its own database — automated, personalized B2B outreach to Qatar companies, to convert them into paid users. Two distinct activities need your opinion:

**(A) Sending unsolicited commercial email** to business decision-makers at Qatar companies, at scale (initially ~150/day, scaling later), with full sender identification and one-click unsubscribe.

**(B) The "gift-a-lead" mechanic.** In each email we would include a *matched potential customer* for the recipient, drawn from our database — i.e., a company in Qatar that plausibly needs the recipient's services and shows a public business signal (e.g., newly licensed, hiring, expanding). **Our proposed design (please confirm/adjust):**
- The email **names and describes the matched company** (name, sector, size band, location, and a **factual public signal** such as "recently licensed" or "expanding") plus any **company-level / general** contact (e.g., website, general phone, info@ address).
- The email **does NOT disclose any named individual / decision-maker** at that company, nor any individual's personal contact details.
- The **decision-makers' identities + contacts, and additional similar companies, are revealed only AFTER the recipient signs up to Bell**, at which point they flow through Bell's standard paid "reveal" process, governed by Bell's terms, opt-out and suppression controls.

The intent is that the only personal data of individuals ever disclosed is disclosed *inside* our governed product to a registered user — never broadcast in an unsolicited email.

## 3. Specific questions for counsel

1. **Direct-marketing consent (PDPPL / NCSA).** Qatar appears to require prior opt-in consent for direct electronic marketing. Under what conditions, if any, may Bell lawfully send unsolicited B2B marketing email to (a) a generic business inbox (info@/sales@) and (b) a named individual's work email? What is the safest defensible basis, and what volume/targeting limits should apply?
2. **Company vs personal data.** Is publicly-registered **company** information (name, sector, registered address, general business contact, public status/signals) treated as personal data under PDPPL when it does **not** identify a specific individual? Does our design (gift the company, withhold named individuals) materially reduce risk, and is it sufficient?
3. **Disclosure to a third party.** Is it lawful to include a *named company + factual public signal* (not a named individual) about Business Y inside a marketing email to Business X? What are the risks (PDPPL, defamation, commercial harm) of attaching a "buying signal," and how should we phrase signals to stay safe?
4. **Reveal-after-signup.** Does disclosing the decision-makers' personal contact details **only to a registered, paying Bell user, through Bell's product and terms** (rather than in the cold email) change the analysis? What consent / notice / lawful-basis is required for that in-product disclosure?
5. **Public-register reuse / purpose limitation.** To what extent may Bell lawfully reuse data obtained from Qatar public registers for (a) building its database and (b) marketing? What "purpose limitation" or notice obligations attach?
6. **Mandatory safeguards.** Please confirm our compliance checklist (Section 4) is sufficient, and add anything required: sender identification, one-click unsubscribe, do-not-contact suppression, data-subject rights (access/rectification/erasure/objection), privacy policy contents, record-keeping, and a data-subject complaint process.
7. **Cross-border / non-Qatar recipients.** Any additional requirements (GDPR/CAN-SPAM) if some recipients are outside Qatar, and how should we segment.
8. **Penalties & enforcement.** Realistic exposure (we understand fines may range QAR 1M–5M per violation) and what regulators (NCSA) expect in practice.
9. **Special-category data.** Confirmation that we must exclude special-nature data (health, religion, ethnicity, etc.) from any processing/marketing.

## 4. Compliance measures Bell already has / will build (please validate)

- **Sender identification** — real legal name + Doha physical address in every email.
- **One-click unsubscribe** (List-Unsubscribe header) + opt-out honored immediately.
- **Global do-not-contact suppression** — already built (hard bounces/complaints auto-suppress); will extend to cover all unsubscribe/objection/erasure requests.
- **Email verification** before sending (reduces misdirected mail).
- **Data-subject rights workflow** — access, rectification, erasure, objection to marketing — easy and free.
- **Published privacy policy** disclosing data sources (incl. public registers), purposes, recipients, and rights.
- **Records** of lawful basis / suppression / disclosures.
- **Marketing clearly labelled**; honest subject lines and headers.
- **Reveal-after-signup** for all named-individual contact data, inside the product, under Bell's terms.

## 5. What we need from you

A written opinion on questions 1–9, a clear **go / adjust / stop** on the gift-a-lead design in Section 2(B), any mandatory changes to Section 4, and (if useful) a reviewed privacy policy + outreach disclaimer. We will not begin outreach until your sign-off.
